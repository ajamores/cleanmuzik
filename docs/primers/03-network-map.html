The downloading aspect

The journey of one song

You paste a YouTube link. Here's every hop it makes — notice that steps 2–6 all happen on the server; your device just kicks it off and, later, plays the result.

The two things to remember

Why it's shaped this way

Your devices never touch YouTube. They talk only to your server. The server is the one doing the downloading and identifying — which is why the heavy work happens in one place, sequentially, and why your phone can be anywhere.

Your music only ever lives on your server. Streaming from your phone isn't "the cloud" — it's the Tailscale tunnel reaching back to files that never left home. (That's Primer 01.)
