The one snag — and why it's its own thing

The cover-art helper only works on whole albums.

The tool that fetches artwork was built to decorate a full album — all its songs at once. It flatly refuses a lone, single track. But every song off YouTube arrives as exactly that: a single track, no album around it. So the helper looks at ours and does nothing. It's not broken; it was simply never meant for this.

Picture a framing shop that will happily frame a whole photo album — but hands back a single loose photo and says “come back with the album.” Our songs are always the loose photo. So we hang the cover ourselves — a small, separate job.

That's the whole reason this is a decision and not just a bug: it's a different piece of work from the recognise-and-file engine we just proved. (Lyrics rides along with it — same kind of small follow-up.)
